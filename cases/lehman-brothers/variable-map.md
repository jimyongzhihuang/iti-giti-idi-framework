# Lehman Brothers Variable Map

This file records the preliminary variable map for applying the ITI–gITI–IDI framework to the Lehman Brothers case.

The purpose is to identify which observed data points belong to ITI, gITI, and IDI.

## ITI Variables

ITI variables represent expected institutional strain embedded in formal structure.

Possible variables include:

- leverage ratio;
- asset classification;
- liquidity reserve;
- short-term funding dependence;
- repo exposure;
- balance-sheet concentration;
- regulatory capital position;
- disclosed risk exposure;
- internal risk tolerance;
- auditor-visible financial structure.

## gITI Variables

gITI variables represent realized outcome-side tension after market and institutional reaction.

Possible variables include:

- stock price decline;
- credit spread movement;
- CDS spread movement;
- liquidity withdrawal;
- counterparty reaction;
- rating downgrade;
- market capitalization loss;
- emergency funding pressure;
- bankruptcy timeline acceleration;
- post-failure systemic contagion signals.

## IDI Variables

IDI variables measure the gap between expected strain and realized outcome-side tension.

Possible variables include:

- difference between disclosed risk and market reaction;
- difference between formal capital position and actual liquidity failure;
- difference between reported balance-sheet stability and realized collapse speed;
- difference between regulatory expectation and bankruptcy outcome;
- difference between institutional confidence and counterparty withdrawal.

## Working Formula

IDI = |ITI - gITI|

The formula will be refined after variables are collected, normalized, and tested against the case timeline.
