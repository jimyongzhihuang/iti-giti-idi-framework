# Preliminary Index Logic

This file records the preliminary logic for applying the ITI–gITI–IDI framework to the Lehman Brothers case.

The purpose is to keep the index logic separate from raw data, case description, and final interpretation.

## 1. ITI Layer

The ITI layer represents expected institutional strain before failure.

For the Lehman Brothers case, the ITI layer may be built from formal and disclosed indicators, including leverage, liquidity position, asset concentration, short-term funding dependence, repo exposure, regulatory capital position, disclosed risk factors, and auditor-visible financial structure.

The purpose of ITI is to estimate the level of institutional pressure that was visible or formally embedded before collapse.

## 2. gITI Layer

The gITI layer represents realized outcome-side tension.

For the Lehman Brothers case, the gITI layer may be built from observed market and institutional outcomes, including stock price decline, credit spread movement, CDS spread movement, rating downgrade, counterparty withdrawal, liquidity stress, bankruptcy timeline acceleration, and post-failure systemic contagion signals.

The purpose of gITI is to extract the actual geometric shape of institutional stress after the system reacted.

## 3. IDI Layer

The IDI layer measures the gap between ITI and gITI.

IDI is calculated as:

IDI = |ITI - gITI|

In this case, IDI will be used to examine whether the expected institutional strain was smaller than, larger than, or structurally misaligned with the realized outcome-side tension.

## 4. Normalization

Before calculation, variables must be normalized so that ITI and gITI can be compared.

Possible normalization steps include:

- converting variables into comparable index scores;
- assigning each variable to a timeline period;
- separating balance-sheet indicators from market indicators;
- distinguishing disclosed institutional expectation from realized market reaction;
- recording the source for each variable.

## 5. Timeline Alignment

The Lehman Brothers case requires timeline alignment.

The same period should not mix pre-failure expectation data with post-failure outcome data unless the comparison is clearly marked.

A basic working structure may be:

- pre-failure period;
- immediate crisis period;
- bankruptcy filing period;
- post-failure systemic reaction period.

## 6. Working Output

The intended output is a preliminary Institutional Distortion Index showing the gap between formal institutional expectation and realized systemic outcome.

This output may later be converted into tables, charts, and a short applied case report.
